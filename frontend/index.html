The AI Risk Predictor

Can we predict negative backlash before an ad campaign goes live? This tool utilizes a Logistic Regression model trained on historical YouTube data.

By inputting estimated early-stage volume metrics (views and comments), the model calculates the probability of the video crossing a critical 5% Dislike Ratio threshold.

Business Application

If a video is flagged as "High Risk", platforms or advertisers can preemptively pull programmatic ad spend to protect brand safety, or creators can disable comments/ratings to mitigate algorithmic suppression.

Defining the Baseline:
Dislike Ratio Distribution

Before building predictive models, we must understand standard audience behavior. This histogram plots the frequency of Dislike Ratios across 37,000+ videos.

Notice the severe right-skew: the vast majority of videos maintain a highly positive reception (a ratio close to 0%).

Why the 5% Threshold?

Because organic backlash is exceptionally rare, any video with a dislike ratio exceeding 5% lands in the extreme "long tail" of the distribution. This statistical anomaly indicates organized reviewing bombing, controversial subject matter, or clickbait, making it an ideal threshold for our target variable.

What Drives Backlash?
Correlation Analysis

This chart maps the Pearson Correlation Coefficient of various engagement metrics against our target variable: the Dislike Ratio.

We observe that raw interactions (Total Views, Likes) have surprisingly little correlation with the *ratio* of dislikes. A highly viewed video is not necessarily a highly disliked one.

The Comment Phenomenon

Notice the strongest positive correlation exists between Comments and Dislike Ratio. Highly polarizing or negative content drives debate, inflating the comment section at a rate disproportional to likes. This justifies our model's heavy reliance on early comment volume.

Categorizing Demand:
K-Means Clustering

Beyond risk prediction, how can we categorize content "success"? We applied unsupervised K-Means Clustering (k=3) to plot videos logarithmically by Views vs. Likes.

  • Cluster 0 (Red): Average / Baseline Performers
  • Cluster 1 (Green): High Demand / Viral Hits
  • Cluster 2 (Blue): Low Engagement / Niche

Strategic Value

By segmenting the inventory automatically, platforms can dynamically establish pricing tiers for Ad-Sense. Advertisers can bid premiums for placement exclusively on "Cluster 1" viral hits to guarantee maximum ROI.
